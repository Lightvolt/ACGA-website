  • The Collegiate Go Association is a national network of schools, students, and community members dedicated to strengthening the American Go community.

    From its beginnings as an informal group of New England collegiate clubs, the CGA has grown to include dozens of groups across the United States so players can share the wonderful game of Go.

    If you're looking for a way to connect with other Go players, improve your skills, or help lead the growth of Go in the United States, the Collegiate Go Association is for you!


    The Goals of Collegiate Go


    Spurred by support from the Ing Foundation and the American Go Association, the CGA has big plans for the future of Go in colleges and universities. The time has never been better for national collegiate tournaments, seminars and simul matches against professional players, and the development of a strong Go culture in the United States.

    So what are you waiting for?

    Join the Collegiate Go Association and help us share the fun and challenge of Go!


    New to Go?


    Go is an ancient two-player board game based on simple rules that lead to immense strategic and aesthetic complexity. Also known as weiqi (China), baduk (Korea), or igo (Japan), Go combines the tactical intensity of chess with abstract, global judgment to make an exceptionally challenging and rewarding game. In fact, Go is one of few remaining games where skilled human players can easily defeat the best computers. To learn how to play, take a look at the basics, check out our go-playing images, find a club near you, or contact us with your questions.
