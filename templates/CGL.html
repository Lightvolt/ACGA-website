Season One Championship Tournament

We are excited to announce the Collegiate Go League Season One Championship Tournament! All members of the ACGA are invited to participate. The first season of the CGL will culminate in this single-elimination no-handicap team tournament, and eligibility will be strictly limited to current full-time undergraduate and graduate students. The first two rounds will take place on April 14, and the semi-finals and championship match will be the following week, April 21. The format will be the same as league games, but we will allow matches to be re-scheduled if there are conflicts. Teams must register for the tournament and submit their tournament roster no later than April 9.

Prizes

Cash prizes:

  • 1st Place: $250
  • 2nd Place: $150
  • 3rd Place: $100
These prizes will go to school clubs, or can be used to buy books or other equipment through the ACGA. Additionally we will likely be able to offer memberships to the KGS Insei League, which is a fantastic way for players of all levels to improve!

If you are currently registered for the CGL, you will receive information about how to participate in the CGL Season One Championship Tournament.

League information

The CGL is the first online competitive league for college Go clubs, hosted on KGS, one of the most popular online go servers. Each school fields a team of five players who compete against another school every two weeks, on Saturdays at 1pm EST / 10am PST. Games are handicapped, and hosted in the Collegiate Go League club room on KGS. After the matches, strong players will assist in reviewing games, helping kyu and dan-level players alike to improve.

Participate in our next match.

  • Location: KGS, Room: "Collegiate Go League", under "Club rooms".
  • Time: Matches occur every other Saturday throughout the regular season, starting at 1pm EST, and ending by 3pm EST. Please arrive half an hour earlier than the match start, to minimize delays.
  • Match Format: A "match" refers to a set of five games between two schools. Each school will field five players. The five players from each team will play each other, sorted from strongest to weakest. The match will be won by the school who wins best of the five boards. No-shows for a board are counted as forfeits.
  • Game settings: 30m main time, 5x30second byo-yomi, Chinese Ruleset with 6.5 points komi, unrated, handicapped according to KGS ratings, no undos. A moderator will be on hand to resolve any disputes/conflicts.
  • Team logistics: Each school will nominate a team captain, who will maintain a roster of active team members. These team members should be active members of the club. There is no limit to the number of people on a roster, although only five team members may represent a school in a match. People are eligible to compete for a college club if they are active members of the club.

Join the League

Is your college Go club interested in competing in the next round of the Collegiate Go League?

Your club should designate a team captain, who will be responsible for managing the team. The team captain will be our contact to your club, will regularly update the school's roster, and will coordinate players' login and participation during matches.

Your team captain should then contact us on behalf of your school's Go club, with the following information:

  • The names and ranks (KGS preferred) of 5 or more players. You can have as many players on your roster as you like, but only 5 people may represent your school in any given match. Each team member should be an active member of that school's Go club. During the championship matches, participation will be further restricted to current undergraduate and graduate students.
  • Club info: Club president, club mailing list/contact info., club website, Facebook group, etc. (We want to make finding a local club as easy as possible.)

We'll add your club to our database, and send you more details on how to participate in the next round of the CGL.

The final round of league matches before the end-of-season tournament will be on March 31. To compete in the tournament your school must play in at least one league game, therefore the deadline to sign up for the remainder of season one is March 27.
